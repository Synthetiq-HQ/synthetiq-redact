# Human Review Policy

Synthetiq Redact assists redaction review. It does not make final legal disclosure decisions.

## Mandatory Review

Documents must be reviewed by an authorised staff member when any of the following applies:

- Handwriting is present.
- Average OCR confidence is below the approved pilot threshold.
- Safeguarding, children, health, legal, social-care, or vulnerable-person content is detected.
- The document type is unknown or mismatched.
- Redaction confidence is low.
- The reviewer or system flags uncertainty.
- The release is FOI, SAR, EIR, complaint, legal, social-care, or safeguarding related.

## Reviewer Responsibilities

- Inspect the original and redacted view.
- Approve, reject, modify, or add redactions.
- Capture a reason for rejected or modified redactions.
- Confirm that final release is appropriate under council policy.
- Export the redacted file and audit evidence.

## Product Responsibilities

- Show confidence and reason signals.
- Keep field labels visible where possible.
- Route uncertain cases to review.
- Preserve audit records.
- Avoid wording that implies automatic compliance.

## Pilot Rule

During pilot, no document containing handwriting or high-risk categories should be auto-released.
